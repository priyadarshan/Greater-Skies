About Greater Skies

Greater Skies started as an online repository of things I wanted to explain to my children. There's lots of interesting things out there, many relationships to realize, many insights to delight with. I want them to find the fascination of the sudden comprehension, and I also want them to have a sense of where they are: how big things are, how do they work, how do they interrelate, where does this earth they live on belong.

I am not only talking to them now, at their 7 and 9 years: I want to explain everything I would like to tell them about, regardless of how complex it is, so that they will understand it when the time comes. I try to be clear, but I do not dumb things down. This is not child talk.

Many of the things I intend to talk about were first presented to me in needlessly complicated or boring ways. I am making an effort to find the clearest explanations, those that make possible the flash of insight, but I am bound to fail, as I am my only yardstick: please do tell me if you find something that's wrong, badly told, or that can be explained in a better way.

There are more articles in Spanish than in English. This is because Spanish is my first language my children don't speak English, but I would like to end up with everything in at least the two languages.

I would like Greater Skies to grow into a collaborative project. It is hosted at github, and I've set it up so that it should be easy to add articles and translations. So far only English and Spanish are represented, but I'd like to add other languages. If you want to contribute you can do it in two ways: write things down and send them to me, or fork the git repository at github, add whatever you deem convenient, and let me know.

Juan Reyero, 2010-01-17
